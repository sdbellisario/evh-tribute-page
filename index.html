"A tribute page to EVH"

Eddie Van Halen

One of rock music's most innovative guitarists.

Eddie Van Halen performing on stage.

Edward Lodewijk Van Halen was one of the most creative and orginal guitarists in rock music history. His wild and adventurous playing style came to define hard rock guitar in the 1980s and beyond. With the band Van Halen (featuring his brother Alex on drums, Michael Anthony on bass guitar and David Lee Roth on vocals) they put of record after record of high intensity party music. Sammay Hagar joined in 1985 to replace Roth as the lead singer and their sound turned to more commercial oriented in the latter half of the decade and darker material in the 1990's. Gary Cherone briefly joined on vocals in 1998 for one record before the band went on a hiatus. 2004 saw a reunion with Hagar and finally a Roth reunion in 2007 leading towards their final record in 2012. While Eddie sadly passed away in October 2020, his music and legacy live on through the ears and fingers of eager and determined guitarists the world over.

Hallmarks of EVH's guitar style:

  • Two-handed tapping
  • Using a Variac unit to crank up the voltage on the amp for maximum volume
  • Effects like delay, phase shifter, and vibrato bar divebombs
  • Use of the B string for major chords lending a melodic quality uncommon in hard rock at the time
